Debrief 3:

Aliens from Outer Space! Extraterrestrial life

	Perhaps ever since the moment humanity has gained consciousness, we have wondered whether intelligent life exists, deep in the ground, in the ocean, in the sky, and even in other planes of existence.

	However, as far as we currently know, we have not been able to measure signs of other intelligent life in the universe. This, according to Enrico Fermi, is a paradox that exemplifies the conflict between the probability of intelligent life and the total lack of evidence for it having arisen anywhere but earth.

	There are multiple potential explanations for this, such as the principal of mediocrity that argues Earth is not unusual in having intelligent life, where by contrast, the Rare Earth hypothesis postulates that our existence is due to a series of events unlikely to occur together.

	Unfortunately, most of the potential answers we currently have to not seem to be optimistic. In 1966, Robin Hanson introduced the concept of The Great Filter, which refers to a phenomenon in which there exists a nearly impenetrable barrier to development that makes detectable intelligent extraterrestrial life in the universe rare, due to extinction events that end life before it can become space-faring.

	For example, the Dark Forest Hypothesis posits that Extraterrestrial civilizations may exist but need to remain hidden out of fear of a more advanced and hostile civilization annihilating them. Perhaps even more terrifying is the possibility of the Berserker Hypothesis, in which self replicating machines are sent throughout the galaxy to locate and destroy any competing intelligent life before it becomes a threat.
