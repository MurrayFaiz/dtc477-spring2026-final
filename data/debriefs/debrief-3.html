Aliens!

Essential Information:

Perhaps ever since the moment humanity has gained consciousness, we have wondered whether intelligent life exists, deep in the ground, in the ocean, in the sky, and even in other planes of existence.

According to Enrico Fermi, the Fermi paradox exemplifies the conflict between the probability of intelligent life and the total lack of evidence for it having arisen anywhere but earth.

There are two main Concepts to explain this: the principal of mediocrity that argues Earths's conditions for life are common in the universe, and the Rare Earth hypothesis that it only exists due to a rare combination of unlikely events.

In 1966, Robin Hanson introduced the concept of The Great Filter, a nearly impenetrable barrier making intelligent extraterrestrial life in the universe rare, due to extinction events that end life before it can become space-faring.

There are also a few more potential answers to this in the Dark Forest Hypothesis, where intelligent life must hide to avoid annihilation, or the Berserker Hypothesis where Von Neumann probes seek out life and attack extraterrestrial life regardless of threat.
